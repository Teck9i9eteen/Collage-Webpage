Job Description:

Create new dance routines. Rehearse performance of routines. May direct and stage presentations.Education and training requirements vary with the type of dancer; however, all dancers need many years of formal training. Nearly all choreographers began their careers as dancers.

Rewarding Aspect of Career:

Pay for choreography work is typically low aside from the most visible professionals in the industry. Choreographers earned a median hourly wage of just $18.11 in 2010, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. Pay in more prestigious dance studios in major markets such as Chicago and New York is higher. Additionally, some choreographers own their own dance studios and may earn higher income operating their business and providing lessons.

The Inside Scoop: A day in the life of a Choreographer

  1. Early Morning: Rehearsal
  2. mid-morning: get a tour of the environment where the performance is suppose to take place, start heading back to studio.
  3. Lunch/Afternoon: Create choreography for the dancers and performers, Practice the newly created choreography with the dancers and practice
  4. Evening: Meet the rest of the dancers and prepare for the dancers to dance with your choreography

Job Responsibilities

After the routine is created, the choreographer teaches the steps to the performers at rehearsals. Choreographers often demonstrate routines by performing the dance themselves. After showing the dancers the steps, the choreographer helps them perfect the movements, technique and timing.

Where do they work?

Choreographers work on set for films, on stage for musical productions, with dance companies or at performing arts schools. They coordinate all dance routines and physical movements associated with a performance -- working with dancers, actors, directors and producers, among other production staff.

Expectations/Sacrifices Necessary

Dancers with Commitment will do absolutely anything that is necessary for them to perform at their highest level. They utilize every technique and resource available to them that may enhance their performances. Committed dancers are constantly looking for new ways to develop themselves in all of the areas that contribute to performance.

Employment Projection

Overall employment of dancers and choreographers is projected to grow 4 percent from 2016 to 2026, slower than the average for all occupations. ... However, dancers and choreographers face intense competition, and the number of applicants is expected to vastly exceed the number of job openings.
